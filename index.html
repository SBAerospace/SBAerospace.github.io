What We Do

Built entirely by students.

Every part of the rocket (and everything inside it) is designed, built, and flown by SBHS students. No kits.

01 // Rocketry

Rocketry

We design custom airframes and fin geometries, utilizing OpenRocket simulations to verify stability margins and predict flight paths before manufacturing.

02 // Electronics

Electronics

Custom PCBs, avionics, and the Rocket++ flight computer, designed in KiCad and programmed in C. We build the hardware that goes inside the rocket.

03 // Mechanical

Mechanical

CAD-driven design in SolidWorks and Fusion 360. FEA on critical structures, composite layups, and 3D printed hardware: everything starts with a drawing.

04 // Software

Software

Flight simulations, ground station GUIs, and tools like CircuitSpace, an open-source circuit simulator our electronics team built and uses in-house.

Recent Work

Some of what we've built

System // 01

Active Airbrake System

Servo-driven airbrakes that autonomously deploy mid-flight to hit a target apogee. PID control loop, Kalman filter, and a custom PCB, all designed in-house.

Avionics // 02

Rocket++

Our custom flight computer. Handles dual-deploy ejection charge firing, flight state detection, and telemetry logging to onboard flash memory.

Competition // 03

American Rocketry Challenge

We compete in ARC, one of the biggest student rocketry competitions in the country. Our rocket has to hit a target altitude and duration with a raw egg payload onboard.
