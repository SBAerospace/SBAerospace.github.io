About Our Aerospace Team

Pioneering the future of aerospace engineering through student innovation

Dedicated to Aerospace Excellence Since 2023

Our South Brunswick Aerospace Team consists of passionate STEM students dedicated to exploring the frontiers of aerospace engineering. Founded in 2023, we've rapidly grown from a small group of rocket enthusiasts to a competitive team representing South Brunswick High School at national aerospace competitions.

We specialize in designing and building advanced UAVs, competition rockets, and innovative aerospace solutions. Our multidisciplinary team combines expertise in mechanical engineering, computer programming, physics, avionics, and CAD design to create cutting-edge aerospace projects including our revolutionary airbrakes system.

Through collaborative teamwork, scientific dedication, and creative problem-solving, we strive to push the boundaries of what high school aerospace teams can achieve in competitive rocketry and aerospace engineering.

Our Aerospace Engineering Projects

Innovative solutions in rocketry, avionics, and aerospace technology

Precision Airbrakes System

Our innovative airbrakes implementation enables precise altitude targeting with high accuracy during American Rocketry Challenge competitions, featuring advanced aerodynamic design and real-time control systems.

CircuitSpace Avionics Planner

Our custom-built circuit planning software designed specifically for rocketry teams to easily design, plan, and visualize complex avionics setups and electrical systems for competition rockets.

Rocket++ Control Library

Our proprietary C++ software library developed to streamline the configuration and control of airbrakes, recovery systems, and other critical rocket subsystems with efficient, reliable code.

Competition Rocket Build

Our fully assembled competition rocket representing hundreds of hours of engineering design, precision manufacturing, and rigorous testing by our dedicated aerospace team members.

Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)

Advanced CFD analysis and simulation to optimize drag characteristics and flight dynamics of our rocket systems, ensuring peak performance and safety in competitive launches.

Our Specialized Aerospace Teams

Three expert divisions driving innovation in competitive rocketry and aerospace engineering

⛽

Liquid Propulsion Team

Developing advanced small-scale liquid rocket engines using gaseous oxygen and hydrocarbon fuels for independent research, propulsion testing, and static fire demonstrations.

🚀

American Rocketry Challenge Team

Competing in America's largest student rocketry competition with over 5,000 participants nationwide. Top performing teams launch near Washington D.C. and present their innovations at Capitol Hill.

🌌

High Power Rocketry Team

Engineering advanced rockets exceeding 160+ newton-seconds impulse, featuring dynamic control surfaces, sophisticated avionics packages, and specialized scientific payloads for research missions.
