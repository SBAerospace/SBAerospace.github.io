Our Mission

We're not just a rocket club.

SBAerospace was founded in 2023 by a small group of SBHS students who wanted to actually build things, not just watch videos about them. We started with 10 people, a lot of ambition, and very little budget.

The goal is simple: give SBHS students a real place to engineer. That means actual rocketry builds, custom PCBs, hands-on CAD, and software that eventually ends up flying at 10,000 feet per second.

We compete in the American Rocketry Challenge and work on original projects year-round. Upperclassmen teach freshmen, everyone learns something new every meeting, and at the end of the season we go to a field and launch a rocket we built ourselves.

Our Story

How we got here

2023

Founded

Ten students, zero budget, one goal: build a real rocket. We started meeting after school and figure things out as we went.

2023-2024

First builds & early flights

First rocket builds, first launches. We got our hands on motors, built airframes, and started learning what actually breaks during a flight and what doesn't.

2024

Electronics & software sub-teams formed

We formalized dedicated sub-teams. Rocket++ (our in-house flight computer) started development. CircuitSpace was built and open-sourced on GitHub.

2025

ARC competition season

Competing in the American Rocketry Challenge for the first time with a full custom build: airbrake system, Rocket++ avionics, and a lot of OpenRocket sims.
