Sub-Teams

Four teams.
One rocket.

Every part of what we build is owned by a dedicated sub-team. Here's what each one does and what you'd learn by joining.

Rocketry Team

Design & Launch

The rocketry team designs and builds everything that leaves the ground: airframes, nose cones, fins, and recovery systems. They're responsible for translating simulation data into physical hardware and getting the rocket to the pad safely.

Certifications
TRA L1 target
Main tool
OpenRocket
Materials
G12 FG, plywood
Competition
ARC 2025

Electronics Team

Avionics & PCB

The electronics team designs and builds the hardware that goes inside the rocket. That means custom PCBs, flight computers, sensor arrays, and telemetry systems. They also write the low-level firmware that makes all of it work.

PCB tool
KiCad
MCU
RP2040 / STM32
Main project
Rocket++
Firmware lang.
C / MicroPython

Mechanical Team

CAD & Fabrication

The mechanical team owns the structural side of everything we build. They CAD every component before it gets made, run FEA on critical parts, and do the actual fabrication, whether that's machining, 3D printing, or hand-laying composites.

CAD
SolidWorks, Fusion
Fab methods
FDM, composites
Analysis
FEA, hand calcs
Current focus
Airbrake system

Software Team

Simulations & Tools

The software team builds the tools the whole club depends on: flight simulators, data visualization pipelines, ground station GUIs, and open-source utilities. They also support the electronics team with embedded firmware when needed.

Languages
Python, JS, C++
Sim tools
RASAero, OpenRocket
Main project

GitHub
How We Work

Separate teams,
shared rocket.

Each sub-team has its own meetings, leads, and projects, but everything eventually integrates. A fin designed by mechanical has to fit around wires run by electronics. A PID loop written by software has to talk to a servo mounted by mechanical.

We do cross-team reviews before finalizing any design, and hold weekly all-hands meetings to keep everyone on the same page. There's a lot of overlap, and that's the point.

Weekly Design Reviews

Every team presents progress. Leads flag integration concerns early before anything gets built.

Cross-team mentorship

Electronics teaches mechanical to solder. Software teaches rocketry to read sensor data.

Shared documentation

Every design file, datasheet, and meeting note lives on our shared GitHub org.

Want to join a team?

We take new members every semester. You don't need experience, just curiosity and the willingness to show up.
